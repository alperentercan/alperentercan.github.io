Alperen Tercan

Hello there! I am a research intern at Max Planck Institute for Software Systems in Dr. Adish Singla's Machine Teaching group. I have recently completed a master's in Computer Science at Colorado State University, where I worked on Reinforcement Learning and Formal Methods under the supervision of Prof. Vinayak Prabhu and Prof. Chuck Anderson. My thesis topic was "Solving MDPs with Thresholded Lexicographic Ordering Using Reinforcement Learning".

My research goal is to build intelligent systems that can reliably solve challenging real-world problems. In my research, I like developing intuitive conceptual ideas into such systems through rigorous theoretical analysis. My main research themes have been developing neuro-symbolic systems that combine deep learning with symbolic reasoning and bringing Reinforcement Learning (RL) closer to real-world applicability. To this end, I developed novel methods that improve the sample efficiency of RL in control and program synthesis. Moreover, I designed approaches that allow more intuitive specifications for RL when describing user preferences over multiple objectives.

Previously, I graduated with a B.S in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from Bilkent University, Turkey.
